# Research Note

## Working study title

Coding issue domains and ambiguity in GLES most-important-problem responses

## Short motivation

Open-ended most-important-problem responses are often short issue mentions, but they still require interpretive choices about issue domain, specificity, framing, and ambiguity. This makes them a useful first test case for OpenCodebook as an interpretive coding workflow engine rather than only a demo script.

## Near-term study goals

- inspect the available open-ended response material
- define a first pilot sample
- refine the GLES MIP codebook
- prepare future reliability and disagreement analysis
